Understanding Sciatica

Sciatica is a common pain that shoots down the path of the sciatic nerve, which runs from your lower back to your legs. Usually, it hits just one side of your body. Remember, sciatica is actually a sign of another health problem, not a condition on its own. It could be due to a slipped disc or a narrow spine.

If you're feeling this kind of pain, don't just wait for it to pass. It's key to see a doctor early on to stop things from getting worse and to keep yourself safe. Ignoring the pain isn't the way to go. Take charge of your well-being and get in touch with a healthcare pro as soon as you can.

Diagnostic Approaches

As a chiropractor specializing in sciatica, my approach to your care begins with a thorough diagnostic process. It's crucial to pinpoint the exact cause of your discomfort to tailor the most effective treatment plan

Chiropractic Diagnostic Techniques for Sciatica

When you come to me with sciatica symptoms, I'll start with a comprehensive physical examination. This includes a detailed assessment of your spine and legs, focusing on the functionality of your nerves and muscles. As a chiropractor, I pay close attention to spinal alignment and look for any signs of nerve interference or muscular tension that could be contributing to your sciatica.

Utilizing Advanced Imaging in Chiropractic Care

In some cases, further diagnostic tools such as MRI or CT scans might be necessary. These imaging techniques provide clear, detailed views of the internal structures of your body, allowing us to identify any disc issues or bone spurs that might be impinging on your nerves. In my practice, I integrate these advanced diagnostic methods with a chiropractic understanding of spinal health to ensure a comprehensive evaluation.
